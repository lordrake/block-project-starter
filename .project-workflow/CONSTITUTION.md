# Constitution

## Mission

- Help teams start CMS-backed websites with a clear, scalable block-based content model that can grow from simple pages into richer digital experiences.
- Demonstrate how reusable page elements, localization, editorial content, and frontend presentation can work together in a maintainable starter.

## Target Users

- Frontend developers building or evaluating Optimizely CMS-backed websites.
- Digital experience teams that need a practical foundation for reusable page composition.
- Product and content teams who need multilingual page structures that remain predictable as sites grow.

## Core Outcomes

- Teams can quickly understand how to assemble pages from reusable blocks and page elements.
- Developers can extend the starter without losing consistency across content types, styling, stories, and localized routes.
- Editors and stakeholders can see a realistic model for composing pages across English and Italian experiences.
- The starter gives enough structure for production-minded evaluation while staying small enough to learn from.

## Product Principles

- Composition first: pages should be built from reusable, understandable blocks rather than one-off templates.
- Editorial clarity: content structures should make page assembly predictable for teams working across roles.
- Localization ready: multilingual support should feel like a core capability, not a later retrofit.
- Demonstrable quality: examples should be complete enough to show intended usage, states, and extension paths.
- Starter discipline: the project should remain approachable, focused, and easy to adapt.

## Non-Goals

- Become a full production website for a specific brand, campaign, or vertical.
- Optimize for every CMS integration pattern or content modeling strategy.
- Serve as a broad component library unrelated to CMS-driven page composition.
- Prioritize visual novelty over clear architecture, reusable content patterns, and practical evaluation.
